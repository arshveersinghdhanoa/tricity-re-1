Milestone 2: Data Pipeline Discovery & Report
Overview & Goal
Milestone 2 is a paid discovery deliverable focused on research and a written assessment . Its core goal is to provide a clear, written go/no-go on the automated data pipeline before any build effort is sunk into development .

⚠️ Critical Mindset: Reporting that a portal is legally or technically not viable to scrape counts as a successful Milestone 2, not a failure .

What to Do (Acceptance Criteria)
To successfully complete this milestone, the following explicit deliverables must be achieved:

Written ToS/robots.txt Review: Conduct a legal review for both PSRERA (rera.punjab.gov.in) and GMADA (gmada.gov.in) to explicitly state whether automated public-record access is permissible .
Technical Inspection Results: Document the results of running the repository's built-in --inspect mode against both portals . This must detail the live site structure and identify if any anti-bot or scraping mitigation measures exist .
Source Recommendations: Provide a formal recommendation for each source, categorizing it as either: (a) buildable as designed, (b) buildable with stated changes/effort, or (c) not viable as designed with a proposed alternative fallback plan (e.g., manual entry workflow, alternative source, or data partnership) .
Milestone 3 Scoping: Deliver an accurate effort and time estimate for the Milestone 3 build phase based directly on these technical findings .
How to Do It (Execution Strategy)
Legal Review First: Analyze external terms and guidelines before conducting wide-scale automated collection .
Execute Codebase Inspection: Run the repository pipeline scripts using the --inspect flag to map the live portals and gauge technical responsiveness .
Document Staging Rows: Ensure any exploratory parsing tests output to local or staging configurations only .
Draft the Synthesis Report: Compile all findings, viability grades, and follow-up estimates into a final text brief for Client sign-off .
Technical & Non-Negotiable Requirements
No Production Scraping: Absolutely no automated production scraping or massive collection jobs are permitted before this review is complete and formally approved .
Staging Writes Only: Scrapers and test data pipelines must never write directly to production tables . All operations must strictly follow the staging → validation → promotion architecture .
Polite-Scraping Compliance: Any network testing during inspection must keep polite-scraping configurations completely intact, including enforced rate limits, honest User-Agents, and running calls during off-peak windows .
Next Steps
Once the Client approves the Milestone 2 written report and confirms the viability of the sources, the project will officially proceed to Milestone 3 (Data Pipeline Build) to execute the approved approach .
